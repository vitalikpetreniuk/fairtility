We Are The
Technology

Using thousands of embryo image and video examples and deep-learning based AI, we developed the world’s most advanced AI-based decision support tool that is able to non-invasively analyze and identify embryos with the highest potential for implantation.
How our tech works
KID+ uses deep convolutional neural networks (CNNs) to analyze time lapse embryo videos, each consisting of several hundreds of images spaced by 10-20 minute intervals. We train our algorithms on an extremely large data-set consisting of ~126,000 embryo videos, many of which are tagged with implantation outcomes. We use this large dataset “to teach” the AI engine meaningful statistical patterns associated with the viability of an embryo.
What is AI?
In simple words, AI (artificial intelligence) is an area of computer science in which machines learn to complete complex tasks and adapt their performance based on past results. Recently, several artificial intelligence (AI)-based methods have emerged as objective, standardized, and efficient tools for evaluating human embryos. As more data inputs are captured, such as medical history, AI is proven in many cases to outperform humans in clinical treatments. The potential value for increased AI usage in medicine is not just a reduction of manual tasks and the freeing up of a physician’s time, but also increasing efficiency and productivity. It empowers the move towards “precision medicine”.

The learning scheme KID+ is based on an optimized fusion of spatial (morphology) and temporal (morphokinetics) machine learning.
Morphological Learning
Embryo and blastocyst morphologies are known to be a predictive marker for implantation success. In current and typical workflows, manual morphology grading is used to select the embryo with the greatest implantation potential. The advent of time lapse incubators and advancements in computer vision have enabled KID+ to track the morphology of developing embryos.
KID+ uses supervised and unsupervised learning algorithms
  • Supervised learning: using Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), KID+ learns how to rank each image in order to optimize
  • implantation prediction outcome
  • Unsupervised learning: using CNN loss-output the CNN’s prediction accuracy is improved
Morphokinetic Learning
Morphokinetic parameters of the embryo are known to be correlated with implantation potential and can be used as predictors of embryo quality. Leveraging video data captured by time lapse incubators, these predictive models, which are based on a combination of statistical analysis and AI methods, can be used to predict embryo implantation. AI is able to identify nuances, better the human eye and learns the subtle parameters associated with each developmental event. KID+ “watches” the embryos as they develop and tracks the timing or kinetics in between each developmental event. Because KID+ has been trained on an extensive dataset and has learned how morphokinetic features found in each frame are associated with what outcomes, it uses timing between each event to calculate the likelihood of blast formation and implantation.
Trained on frames of each developmental event, the system’s Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) learn:
  1. Timing of morphokinetic events / development stages
  2. Implantation probability
